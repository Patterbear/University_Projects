Significant Processors - Index

By Group 16

This website was created by Computer Science Group 16 at the University of Leicester, and its purpose is to teach visitors about computer processors considered to be significant in the history of computing.

Computer processors have (quite literally) been at the core of the technological revolution. From the first ever general purpose CPU, the Intel 4004, to the latest range of high-performance modern CPUs like Ryzen and Intel i series, this site aims to explore every significant computer processor in history. The evolution of computer processors has allowed manufacturers to develop and produce more sophisticated and capable computers, exponentially expanding the possibilities of computation. In a span of around 40 years, processors have evolved from simple 4-bit processing units to the complex powerhouses we see today, which are used for gaming, word processing and so much more. There have been many milestones in this journey from the primitive 4004 to the modern-day Ryzen processors, and this website will allow you to explore the unique history behind each significant processor in Computing History.

While it would be quite the undertaking to compile a list of all the processors ever created and their individual impacts on computing history, we have managed to create a list of what we consider some of the most important processors in computing history, with a page describing each unique processor, its development and the impact it had (or has) on the world of computing.

Every page of this website contains a detailed report with corresponding media detailing a certain processor (or range of processors) and its impact on the computing world.
