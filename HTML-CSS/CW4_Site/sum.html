Significant Processors - Summary

By Group 16

Why did we choose CPUs for our project? - CPUs have been instrumental in the evolution of computing. Early machines like the University of Pennsylvania 's ENIAC required physical rewiring in order for different tasks to be conducted. When the first stored-program computers were created, the first devices that be considered a CPU came with them. A CPU itself is simply a device used to execute computer programs, and with the release of each significant processor came a notable advance for computing as a whole.

We've come quite a way from the very basic CPUs used in the first stored-program computers, with processors now capable of executing billions of instructions in mere seconds across multiple cores.

Each page in this website has been authored by an individual Informatics student at the University of Leicester and contains detailed information and media on processors or ranges of processors that are considered to be signifcant in the history of computing. On each page you will find facts, videos, pictures and audio relating to the processor chosen by the student.

All the processors (or processor ranges) detailed on this site have had their own very remarkable impact on the world of computing, and without them the computing world as we know it today may have looked very different.
